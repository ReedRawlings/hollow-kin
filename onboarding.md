# **Hollow Kin — UI/UX & Player Information Design**

*Working Document — Subject to Change*

---

## **Overview**

Hollow Kin has a lot of overlapping systems — essence, traits, marks, abilities, stars, breeding, capture, relics, and town upgrades. The player needs clear, consistent ways to parse all of it without being overwhelmed. This document covers the onboarding flow to introduce the player to each composite piece of the game.

Two currencies form the spine everything hangs off of. **Obols** are the in-run currency, harvested from every fight and spent during the descent on survival (heals, revives, capture, shop items). **Essence** is the permanent currency: on returning to town, leftover (unspent) Obols convert to Essence, which is spent on permanent levels, traits, marks, depth-jumps, and backpack capacity. The core tension the tutorial must land is **spend-now-vs-keep** — every Obol spent on a heal mid-descent is an Obol that won't convert to Essence, so it's a permanent upgrade you didn't take home. Onboarding introduces Obols in Phase 1 so this tension is felt before it's ever explained.

---
## **Onboarding Flow**

The game walks players through the game in two phases.
Phase 1: Selecting Creatures for a run, Combat, Obols, Capture, Rest, the spend-now-vs-keep choice
Phase 2: Traits, Marks, Breeding (as a choice)

## Phase 1

* Initially player only see's one button on the screen "Descend" clicking it takes them into the tower, but instead of starting a typical run they encounter the old man
* Player encounters an old man who offers to help them descend the tower. He presents them with the option of two sets of three different creatures outlined in the creature-roster.md file
* Old man then battles the player with the three other creatures. Upon defeating the old man the player earns their first **Obols** and fully heals MP and HP. Old man explains Obols are the reward from every fight — the coin the tower hands you on the way down, spent right here to keep your pets alive
* Old man sends the player into their first descent. The tower is one continuous descent (no zones); the first stretch runs floors 1–5 toward a mini-boss
* First encounter is always combat. On defeating it the old man points out the Obols it dropped and suggests the player spend a little of it to **capture** one of the creatures they just battled. This capture always works. This introduces capture, the backpack/inventory, and — crucially — the idea that Obols spent here are Obols that won't make it home
* The next encounter is always a **rest**, where the player can heal MP or HP to full. The old man frames the choice plainly: rest is free here, but the heals and revives they'll buy deeper down cost Obols — and every Obol spent is one that won't convert to permanent Essence when they leave. This is the spend-now-vs-keep tension in miniature
* The player fights through two more encounters on the way down, earning Obols from each. This shows the Obol pool accumulating fight-by-fight and lets the player feel it grow — a pool they'll either burn on survival or carry out to convert


## Phase 2
* After those encounters the player reaches the **mini-boss** at floor 5 (mini-bosses sit every 5 floors; major bosses every 10). Defeating it awards a larger chunk of Obols and, because the player hit an in-run accomplishment threshold, **earns a mark** for their active, living pets
* Old man explains the mark is currently temporary — it fades unless the player later spends Essence back in town to **bind it permanently** onto a pet. Essence buys permanence; the deed itself still had to be done in the tower
* Whether they win or lose against the mini-boss, this first descent ends and the player returns to town. On leaving the tower, their **leftover Obols convert to Essence** at a conversion rate — the permanent currency. The old man drives the conversion home: the Obols they held back instead of spending are exactly what became Essence, so hoarding paid off (a full wipe, though, forfeits the leftover Obols — the push-your-luck edge). He then walks them through what town's essence hub can do: buy permanent **levels** (a pet's starting-level floor rises so it begins future runs above level 1), unlock **traits** at essence thresholds, **bind marks**, buy **depth-jumps** to skip cleared floors, and expand backpack capacity
* There is no longevity clock and no forced retirement — pets live until the player chooses to breed them. So the old man introduces **breeding as a choice**, not an obligation: retiring two parents to produce a single stronger offspring. He walks the player through it — how stars/tiers and traits pass down, marks optionally carry, and, importantly, that a chunk of the parents' invested essence/levels **carries over to the offspring as a jump-start** so the new bloodline isn't starting from zero
* The player breeds two of their creatures (at least one carrying a trait) to feel the trade: two pets become one, but that one begins ahead thanks to the essence carry-over
* This leaves the player with three pets — one bred offspring that starts with a tier and carry-over essence, one captured pet, and the third pet from the starting trio

## Finish Onboarding
* From here the town's essence hub is fully open — the Breeder and Creature Box alongside the essence vendors (Leveler, Trait-keeper, Mark-binder, Gatekeeper, Quartermaster) — and the player can spend their banked essence or immediately start another descent.
