# **Hollow Kin — UI/UX & Player Information Design**

*Working Document — Subject to Change*

---

## **Overview**

Hollow Kin has a lot of overlapping systems — essence, traits, marks, abilities, stars, breeding, capture, relics, and town upgrades. The player needs clear, consistent ways to parse all of it without being overwhelmed. This document covers the onboarding flow to introduce the player to each composite piece of the game.

Essence is the spine everything hangs off of: it is the single currency, harvested from every fight, and spent on permanent levels, traits, marks, depth-jumps, backpack capacity, capture, and in-run survival (heals/revives). The core tension the tutorial must land is **spend-now-vs-bank** — every heal bought mid-descent is a permanent upgrade you didn't take home. Onboarding introduces essence in Phase 1 so this tension is felt before it's ever explained.

---
## **Onboarding Flow**

The game walks players through the game in two phases.
Phase 1: Selecting Creatures for a run, Combat, Essence, Capture, Rest, the spend-now-vs-bank choice
Phase 2: Traits, Marks, Breeding (as a choice)

## Phase 1

* Initially player only see's one button on the screen "Descend" clicking it takes them into the tower, but instead of starting a typical run they encounter the old man
* Player encounters an old man who offers to help them descend the tower. He presents them with the option of two sets of three different creatures outlined in the creature-roster.md file
* Old man then battles the player with the three other creatures. Upon defeating the old man the player earns their first **essence** and fully heals MP and HP. Old man explains essence is the reward from every fight — the one thing the tower gives you, and the one thing you spend on everything
* Old man sends the player into their first descent. The tower is one continuous descent (no zones); the first stretch runs floors 1–5 toward a mini-boss
* First encounter is always combat. On defeating it the old man points out the essence it dropped and suggests the player spend a little of it to **capture** one of the creatures they just battled. This capture always works. This introduces capture, the backpack/inventory, and — crucially — the idea that essence spent here is essence not banked
* The next encounter is always a **rest**, where the player can heal MP or HP to full. The old man frames the choice plainly: rest is free here, but the heals and revives they'll buy deeper down cost essence — every one is a permanent upgrade they won't carry home. This is the spend-now-vs-bank tension in miniature
* The player fights through two more encounters on the way down, earning essence from each. This shows essence accumulating fight-by-fight and lets the player feel the pool grow


## Phase 2
* After those encounters the player reaches the **mini-boss** at floor 5 (mini-bosses sit every 5 floors; major bosses every 10). Defeating it awards a larger chunk of essence and, because the player hit an in-run accomplishment threshold, **earns a mark** for their active, living pets
* Old man explains the mark is currently temporary — it fades unless the player later spends essence back in town to **bind it permanently** onto a pet. Essence buys permanence; the deed itself still had to be done in the tower
* Whether they win or lose against the mini-boss, this first descent ends and the player returns to town with whatever essence they banked. The old man walks them through what town's essence hub can do: buy permanent **levels** (a pet's starting-level floor rises so it begins future runs above level 1), unlock **traits** at essence thresholds, **bind marks**, buy **depth-jumps** to skip cleared floors, and expand backpack capacity
* There is no longevity clock and no forced retirement — pets live until the player chooses to breed them. So the old man introduces **breeding as a choice**, not an obligation: retiring two parents to produce a single stronger offspring. He walks the player through it — how stars/tiers and traits pass down, marks optionally carry, and, importantly, that a chunk of the parents' invested essence/levels **carries over to the offspring as a jump-start** so the new bloodline isn't starting from zero
* The player breeds two of their creatures (at least one carrying a trait) to feel the trade: two pets become one, but that one begins ahead thanks to the essence carry-over
* This leaves the player with three pets — one bred offspring that starts with a tier and carry-over essence, one captured pet, and the third pet from the starting trio

## Finish Onboarding
* From here the town's essence hub is fully open — the Breeder and Creature Box alongside the essence vendors (Leveler, Trait-keeper, Mark-binder, Gatekeeper, Quartermaster) — and the player can spend their banked essence or immediately start another descent.
