# **Hollow Kin — Town**

---

## **Overview**

The town is the player's home base between runs. It exists outside the tower and is unaffected by run resets. Town buildings unlock and expand over time using resources gathered during runs. The town does not directly inflate combat power — it improves logistics, breeding quality, and access. The creatures are always the primary source of power.

---

## **Buildings**

### **Creature Box**

Where creatures are stored when not on a run.

* Starts with a small number of slots just two  
* Expands through upgrades purchased with resources earned during run  
* Creatures in the box do not earn marks or levels passively — they must go on runs  
  * Upgrades can be made to the Creature Box that enable XP share from runs. Over time this XP allows creatures stored in the Creature Box to hit their next star's level cap and unlock a trait. The goal of the creature box is to make sure that there are back up creatures used for breeding when necessary.

### **Leathersmith**

Upgrades the player's carry capacity for runs.

* Increases the number of item slots available in the player's inventory during a run  
* More slots means more Plasm for captures, more stones, more consumables  
* Directly affects how many capture opportunities a player can act on per run  
* Upgraded with resources earned during the run  
* Other upgrades offer guaranteed slots for items that are not lost on run death

### **Enhancer**

Uses Breeding Stones and Breeding Relics to improve breeding outcomes. Breeding Stones are found as loot during runs. Breeding Relics are left behind when a creature retires without a breeding partner.

* Single-use stones and relics — every use is a deliberate choice, not a passive global buff
* Only one stone or relic can be applied per breeding event
* As the Enhancer levels up, new stone types and enhancement options become available
* Functions as an active crafting station — the player chooses what to push into a breed rather than receiving a blanket improvement
* A relic used here substitutes for a parent contribution in trait resolution, allowing the player to choose between the relic trait and a parent trait rather than rolling against the random pool
* A relic can be based on either the retiring creature's active trait or its active mark — the player chooses at retirement

## **Resources**

### **Breeding Stones**

The primary currency of the Enhancer.

* Earned as rewards from combat events during runs and after defeating zone bosses  
* Single use — consumed on application  
* Different stone types influence different aspects of breeding outcomes (specific types TBD during design of Enhancer upgrade tree)

### **Town Resources**

Used to upgrade buildings.

* Gathered as loot during runs — distinct from Breeding Stones  
* Specific resource types and building costs TBD during balancing
