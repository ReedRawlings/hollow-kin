# **Hollow Kin — Town**

---

> **Updated 2026-07-23 (Essence Progression Pivot):** Town is now an **Essence Hub**. The Enhancer and Leathersmith are removed as standalone buildings; their jobs are absorbed by essence vendors below (the Quartermaster preserves the Leathersmith's backpack-capacity role). **Essence is the single permanent currency** — Plasm and Breeding Stones are gone. The old Creature Box passive-XP/level mechanic is retired; the Creature Box is now management-only.

---

## **Overview**

The town is the player's home base between runs. It exists outside the tower and is unaffected by run resets. Town is a hub of "folks" who turn **essence** — the single permanent currency, harvested from every fight — into permanent upgrades and access. The town does not directly inflate combat power on its own; the creatures (and the essence invested in them) are always the primary source of power.

---

## **The Folks (Essence Vendors)**

Essence is earned in the tower and spent here. Every spend except the Creature Box costs essence; essence is permanent and non-refundable once committed to a pet.

| Station | Function | Essence? |
|---|---|---|
| **Creature Box** | View available creatures, manage party | No — management only |
| **Leveler** | Buy permanent levels | Yes |
| **Trait-keeper** | Unlock trait slots / levels | Yes |
| **Mark-binder** | Make an earned mark permanent | Yes |
| **Gatekeeper** | Unlock depth-jumps | Yes |
| **Quartermaster** | Increase backpack capacity (hold items to use on the descent) | Yes |
| **Breeder** | Breed a pair (retire parents, carry essence over to offspring) | Yes |

### **Creature Box**

Where creatures are stored when not on a run. **Management only — no essence spent here.**

* View all available creatures and manage the active party
* Creatures do not earn marks, levels, or XP passively — all progression comes from runs and essence spent at the other stations

### **Leveler**

Buys permanent levels for a pet with essence.

* Raises a pet's permanent starting-level floor (a pet begins each run at its invested level instead of 1)
* Cost per level rises classically, so leveling naturally decelerates

### **Trait-keeper**

Unlocks trait slots and trait levels with essence.

* Trait slots and levels unlock at essence thresholds (not star thresholds)

### **Mark-binder**

Makes an earned mark permanent with essence.

* Marks are still earned through in-run accomplishments; by default an earned mark is temporary
* Spending essence here buys *permanence* for an already-earned mark — not the mark itself

### **Gatekeeper**

Unlocks depth-jumps with essence.

* Purchase a start point at a cleared 5-floor break so future runs begin deeper (e.g. buy floor 10 → start at floor 11)
* Depth-jumps are gated by having cleared that break's boss

### **Quartermaster**

Increases backpack capacity with essence. Preserves the old Leathersmith's job.

* Increases the number of item slots available to carry into a descent (items used during the run)
* More slots means more consumables and capture opportunities per run
* Higher tiers can offer guaranteed slots for items that are not lost on run death

### **Breeder**

Breeds a pair of creatures with essence.

* Both parents are retired on breeding
* Invested essence/levels partially carry over to the offspring as a jump-start, so a bloodline never restarts from zero

---

## **Resource — Essence**

Essence is the single permanent currency and the only resource spent in town.

* Harvested from every fight; total per run scales with the number of battles completed, weighted `normal < mini-boss < major boss`
* One shared wallet is spent *either* on in-run survival (heals, revives, capture) *or* banked and carried back to town for the permanent upgrades above — a real spend-now-vs-bank tradeoff
* Permanent and non-refundable once committed to a pet
